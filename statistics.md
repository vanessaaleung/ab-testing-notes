#### Confidence Interval

_How likely the result is robust and  repeatable_


##### Calculation



*   Variance/Standard Deviation
*   Distribution

Example: A 95% confidence interval

if we repeat the experiment over and over again, we would expect the interval we construct around our sample mean to <span style="text-decoration:underline;">cover the true value</span> in the population <span style="text-decoration:underline;">95% of the time</span>.

#### Hypothesis Testing/Inference

_How likely you results occurred due to chance_



*   Null hypothesis: no difference in control and experiment
*   Alternative hypothesis


##### Two-tailed vs. one-tailed tests

Two-tailed test:



1. A statistically significant positive result
2. A statistically significant negative result
3. No statistically significant difference.

One-tailed test:



1. A statistically significant positive result
2. No statistically significant result

##### A versus A test (A/A test)

Compare control with control to measure the underlying variability



*   Alternative: bootstrapping
    *   Use random samples from each side as a simulated experiment
*   [Calculation](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1JM-G0q2NY3fEDD1V81d9vJHoLVzTJv7_HvBNdM4aKAc/edit?usp=sharing)
    *   Calculate the difference in e.g. click-through-probability between two groups
    *   Calculate the standard deviation of differences and assume the metric is normally distributed
    *   Calculate an empirical confidence interval without making assumptions about the distribution
        *   Sort differences
        *   CI from 2nd smallest to 2nd largest
        *   Best to have 40+ experiment for empirical
